Howie Good

Howie Good, a journalism professor at SUNY New Paltz, is the author of several poetry collections, including most recently Beautiful Decay and The Cruel Radiance of What Is from Another New Calligraphy and Fugitive Pieces from Right Hand Pointing Press. He co-edits White Knuckle Press with Dale Wisely.

Alexandra Naughton

Alexandra Naughton is a writer and runs a zine/small press called BE ABOUT IT. She has two books called I WILL ALWAYS BE YOUR WHORE [love songs for Billy Corgan] and YOU COULD NEVER OBJECTIFY ME MORE THAN I'VE ALREADY OBJECTIFIED MYSELF which are available from Punk Hostage Press. You can find her as @theTsaritsa on Twitter.

Tyler Barton

Tyler Barton is one half of The Triangle, the fiction editor of Third Point Press, and an MFA candidate at Minnesota State University. His published stories can be found at tylerstevenbarton.wordpress.com. Follow him @goftyler.

Jose Rico

Jose Rico thinks it's odd to write about himself in the third person. He once attached dental floss to a lightning bug and flew it around the neighborhood. If he were an amateur snake charmer his nickname would be The Moongoose.

manuel arturo abreu

manuel arturo abreu is a poet/artist from the Bronx. See more work at twigtech.tumblr.com and @Deezius.

Lazzlo Jenkins

Lazzlo Jenkins was born and raised in Northern California, before making my move to the midwest to pursue a BFA in Graphic Design at Columbia College Chicago. They are now living and working in Chicago, stationed up in the Logan Square area.

Catch Business

Catch Business is a young woman and an unknown writer. her work has been featured in Electric Cereal, Be About It, The Bohemyth, Zoomoozophone Review, The Chapess and elsewhere. Her first book 'able to / always will' is forthcoming from Civil Coping Mechanisms. She lives and dies daily in Denver.

Joe Nicholas

Joe Nicholas is an agender evolution of experiments and experiences, as well as the editor of The Screaming Sheep. Their work can be found or is forthcoming in BOAAT, Chiron Review, Found Poetry Review, Fruita Pulp, Weave, and other wonderful magazines. Their chapbook Street Monk is forthcoming from Bottlecap Press. They can be found at 8rainCh1ld.tk.

BAGHEERA

Born of the waters of Lake Minnetonka, BAGHEERA, manifested from the grief of Sar R and Lauren, crawled onto the shore to fulfill their true will: invocation, conjurations, machinations. They are based in Denton, TX. Find them on Soundcloud.

Odjoveno

Eva Cordu is Arubian, grand in her being and in her appearance. As a student for Sociology she is open-minded and helpful. Six years ago she began to practice the discipline Theatre. In Suriname, Paramaribo 2011 she won the Jazz Poetry contest of the Foundation "Let's Do It” since then she excelled herself as a poet under the pseudonym Odjoveno, which stands for “serenity." At the moment she is part of the theatre group Zoals Water (Like Water) and the poetry group Punt! (Dot!) based in the Netherlands, Amsterdam.

Eric Baker

Eric Baker is a real estate agent from the Tri-State area and writer of the award-winning book "Eric Baker: In His Own Words" available through Dostoyevsky Wannabe.

Dalton Day

Dalton Day is a trembling dog person and MFA candidate at The New Writer’s Project, as well as the author of Fake Knife and TANDEM. Dalton’s poems have appeared in PANK, Hobart, and Columbia Poetry Review, among others. Dalton can be found online at myshoesuntied.tumblr.com and twitter.com/lilghosthands!

dom schwab

dom schwab is a genderqueer & gay reader/writer of poetry/prose. dom has most recently been published in: BE ABOUT IT zine's REPEAT Issue, Infinite Scroll Issue 3, & Zoomoozophone Review's Issue 5 for female & gender non-conforming poets. dom lives in Chicago. dom's spirit animal is a sparrow with lilac in its beak.

Lauren Belmore

Lauren Belmore is a "mysterious queer" living in Denton, TX. They like to write, make noise, & talk about The Smiths. In their spare time, they enjoy drinking damn fine cups of coffee with their partner, FBI agent Dale Cooper.

Steve Klepetar

Steve Klepetar's work has received several nominations for the Pushcart Prize and Best of the Net. Recent collections include My Son Writes a Report on the Warsaw Ghetto (Flutter Press) and Return of the Bride of Frankenstein (Kind of a Hurricane Press).

Beyza Ozer

Beyza Ozer founded Probably Crying Review while eating a bagel. Their work has appeared in/is forthcoming from Skydeer Helpking, Voicemail Poems, Luna Luna magazine, & other places. Beyza is the author of GOOD LUCK WITH THE MOON & STARS & STUFF (Bottlecap Press 2015). they are an assistant editor of YesYes Books, an associate editor of Big Lucks Books, & social media coordinator of the Lettered Streets Press. Beyza lives in chicago & on twitter @THEREALEMODAD

Emily Eigner

Emily Eigner lives in Bloomington, Indiana.
