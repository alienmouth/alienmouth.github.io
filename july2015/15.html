Li Bo and the Bending Light by Steve Klepetar

“What’s the most fun you can have
with bending light?” he wants to know

as we sit on the banks of the Rainy River
watching sunbeams break into rainbows.

I shrug, throw some bread out onto the water
and we watch small fish leap and thrash.

Suddenly his face is filled with light,
his hair ablaze with honey-gold.

Then, shadows of clouds and a quick cold
ripple of wind. His hands burn red,

and as the waves shorten all through
the spectrum, orange, green and all the rest,

until they pulse ultraviolet, as he turns
to shield my face from x- and gamma rays.

When clouds pass, air warms around us as
sunshine glides softly over the long river’s

curl. He smiles, his face starlit, his shining
hands soaked, transformed by mystery and joy.
