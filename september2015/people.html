Simon Kindt

Simon Kindt is a Brisbane poet, a teacher and MC of Speedpoets, Brisbane’s longest running spoken word open-mic. He is helping to develop a cross-school network of schools that are collaborating on youth spoken-word projects. In collaboration with Chloë Callistemon, he co-authored the self-published collection air / tide in 2014. In 2015 he self-published the verse novella no revelation and, in collaboration with American poet Bill Moran, is co-author of wreck / age. He really just wants to tell you stories - that’s all.

Ebony Stewart

Ebony Stewart is a writer and spoken-word performing artist poet. In 2012, she coached the Neo-Soul slam team finishing 1st place in Group Piece Finals. In 2013, she coached the Austin Poetry Slam team leading them to 5th place at the National Poetry Slam and coached the TheySpeak Poetry Slam team finishing top 8 in the world at Brave New Voices in 2014. She is the only adult female three-time Slam Champion in Austin Texas and has shared stages with Buddy Wakefield, Derrick Brown and the late Amri Baraka. The published author was recently featured in the “Texas Observer”. Ebony writes because she has to and eats cupcakes for fun.

Taj Bourgeois

Taj Bourgeois is a balloon (1987). His skull is covered in peanut butter, his pineapple has magical sensibilities, and his cat just fly out of air. He enjoys making sculptures that describe poems and poems that describe sculptures. He lives in Portland with his wife and daughter, and works as a Taxi Driver. Prior to this position he briefly attended the Portland Northwest College Of Art. Prior to that he lived in a van in Maui. Prior to that he hitchhiked to NYC and busked at Union Square. He’s going to be a famous artist. He’s also going to die just like you. tajbourgeois.tumblr.com Instagram: @tajbourgeois

Jack Chelgren

Jack Chelgren grew up in the suburbs of Seattle, WA, lived in Middletown, CT, for a year, and now resides in Seattle proper. He has received the Arthur Oberg Poetry Prize and an award from the Academy of American Poets; he was selected by Dara Wier for a scholarship from Factory Hollow Press to attend the Juniper Summer Writing Institute at the University of Massachusetts Amherst. He writes poetry and essays, and plans to pursue a PhD in English or American Studies. For two years he ran a poetry zine called Blind Glass.
