3 Poems

by Sara June Woods



dear hairless chelsea,

Two women are building a house
around a dying animal.
They go to the river
& dig up clay
to make into bricks
they bake in an oven.

The bricks stack up high.
Four, five deep.
The walls are thick.
They leave space for a window.
They leave space for a door.

They finish the house
& the animal says
thank you so much
but I am still dying.

Love,
Sara



dear hairless madeline,

Good afternoon, you are a hairless
ocean. I wanted you to know I am
deep in your dark parts,
in a room I don't recognize except
for the cluster of all god's angels here.
Except for the sound of their
terrible coughing & how it echoes
off the walls. Except for the smell
of their hair & feathers, how it combines
& separates inside me & pulls out
old feelings I hardly recall &
can only name with colors.
The angels here take turns approaching
me where I sit & I hear their confessions,
the informational mistakes
in their words, yes, but also in their smell,
which I have already talked about.
I call it smell because I am dying here,
or will come back here to die.
~eventually~.
It's s thing I found out.
or will soon.
Find out.

What I'm trying to say is
if we don't make wishes
they can't not come true & that
I saw you across the street
while I was buying groceries & I wanted to cry.
Were you carrying me in your beak then?
or is that just the shape
your face makes when you think
about the things we got to tired
to finish after we talked so well
about them.

Blessings,
Sara



dear hairless hairless tiny teacup pig playing a piano,

Your song gives me chills.
& the piano you've got sounds
just like the feeling I had riding
the train into this bright city
for the first time & so often since.
Where did you get your piano?
Because the notes all remind me
of people I know, people I knew,
& of these beautiful intersecting lines
they draw across my field of vision,
these single words that I don't
know the meanings of, but know
the feelings of, & how they move
with me when I turn my head
or walk down the street or kiss
a person other than the one
I wish I was kissing.

Hairless, it feels like you
are putting your whole hands
in my wounds.
It feels like a dream
about your family
where everyone dies.
One you can't stop having,
that strangers quote
at you on the street,
that is projected
on the wall
in your favorite
restaurant.

Pig, can we stop now?
I love your song, it's true,
I can't stop looking at it,
but I am tired of feeling for now.
Let's sleep quietly on our stomachs
& hope everything's changed
by the time we wake up.

Love always,
Sara



"dear hairless hairless tiny teacup pig playing a piano" was previoiusly published in Winter Tangerine Review and "dear hairless madeline" is forthcoming in Bat City Review.
